    📌 Key Insights

    1. ChurnOverall churn stands at 32.2%, with the sharpest drop-off in the first 3 months — early-life churn is the single biggest leak in the funnel.
    2. PlanBasic plan churns at 36.9% vs. just 24.0% for Enterprise — cheaper plans attract less-committed customers.
    3. ReasonMissing features and pricing together account for over 40% of churn reasons — more than support issues.
    4. EngagementCustomers in the lowest usage bracket churn at ~37% vs. 27% for highly engaged users — usage is a leading indicator, not just a symptom.
    5. SupportCustomers with 5+ support tickets churn 36.6% of the time vs. 28.3% for zero-ticket customers — unresolved friction predicts churn.
    6. ChannelPartnership-acquired customers churn least (26.1%); Social Media-acquired customers churn most (35.4%) — acquisition quality varies a lot by channel.

    🎯 Recommendations

    • Fix the first 90 days: Build a structured onboarding flow (checklists, milestone emails, an early check-in call) — most churn happens before month 3.
    • Re-position Basic plan value: Either add a lightweight "starter" feature set that reduces cancellations, or nudge engaged Basic users toward Pro with usage-based prompts.
    • Close the feature gap: Prioritize the most-requested "missing features" from churn exit surveys — this is the #1 stated reason for leaving.
    • Trigger win-back workflows on low usage: Flag accounts whose usage score drops below 50 for proactive outreach, before they reach the cancellation page.
    • Tighten support SLAs: Customers with 3+ tickets are meaningfully more likely to churn — track ticket count as an early-warning signal, not just a support metric.
    • Double down on Partnership & Organic channels: They bring in the stickiest customers; consider reallocating some paid/social budget toward partnerships and referral programs.
